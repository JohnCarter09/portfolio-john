RMS(one) Feature Enhancments & Conceptual Design

What is RMS?

The RMS mission is to create a more resilient and sustainable global society through a better understanding of catastrophic events. From earthquakes, hurricanes, and floods, to terrorism and infectious disease, RMS helps financial institutions and public agencies understand, quantify, and manage risk

The Goal(s): To design and build out new features within the RMS(one) application and to enhance some of the exisiting customer workflows and features.

   The Requirements   
  • The PRD (Product Requirements Document).
  • A great PRD is an independent document which can set the context for anyone who reads it. The internal stakeholders and product teams create these PRD', every team is involved. The designers, engineers, solutions and analytics teams hunker down for a week to come to the best conclusions around scope, budget, and useability. During the creation of the PRDS's we ask key questions. What and why are we building this? How are we going to build this? Will this work?

    GOALS:

  • Design an end-to-end experience with less complicated steps than the current RMS UI. (in my presentation of the comparison prototype below, I cover some of the current UI patterns in the existing application).
  • Allow the users to seemlessly flow from one step to the other while creating a comparison.
  • Conceptualize how a user would be able to drill down into more granular data on a histogram, or graph.
  • Design an end-to-end workflow for the Enhancement of creating a job or running a failed job again.
  • Allow the users to seemlessly flow from one step to the other while accessing more and more data on the dashbaord.

What I Learned

  • Working with product teams can be a very hard thing to do, but a very reqarding thing as well. Coming to a consensus on the best solution to a problem can have its difficulties, but as long as we're keeping the users best interest in mind, the product will start to take shape.

What I Changed

  • A lot of times in the PRD, requirements can be fairly vague...Its our job as designers to seek out the product teams for collaborative feedback. In the existing prototype you will notice the add an subtract buttons on the variation selection. Initially I was unsure how to keep those consistent in the app when going from step 1 to step 2, but after some design critiques with the team we decided it would be best to keep them on the right side of the panel. In some of the earlier sketches we did, I had the buttons change from right to left..

Additional testing

  • Due to the time crunch to get this release out the door, there was not a ton of additional testing done. Initially we did some internal pool testing on 10 current RMs employees. We created some use cases using usability hub we got the results back and decided based on some of the recurring themes that we had to rethink some of our initial decisions. .

Solution

  • After delivering this to the developers, our job was not finished. Some of the features and styles that were designed in our prototypes were not able to be done within scope. We had to sit down with product, and engineers to determine what would be feasible for our latest 1.11 release.
