What is Native Realty?

Native Realty is a real estate agency. Located in Denver Colroado with clients from all over the world

The Goal: To create a useable, informative experience for the end user; to help with lead generation, while maintaining a functional motivating website.

   The Process   

Research

  • COMPETITIVE ANALYSIS
  • As a team, we first analyzed several real estate agencies in the area to discover how Native Realty could set themselves apart from other agencies in the Denver market. Ultimately, we found that while existing real estate agencies offered helpful services like, portal set up, offer holdings, and online inspection services, most of them didnt have previous client testimonials, or the ability to create optomized contracts for their clients within their website which from our research was a crucial part of users needs. Additionally, They failed to use visuals to represent thier homes.
  • INITIAL RESEARCH
  • In order to understand how to design for our target demographic; home buyers, we first conducted research on how potential home buyers currently use real estate websites in their serch process when shopping for a new home. This informed our perception of context as well as the necessity for a well-designed well performing product for this specific demograpic of people.
  • USER INTERVIEWS
  • After screening around 30 participants / potential home buyers from the area for eligibility in our study, we conducted six interviews with people who had bought homes using native's services in the past. We wanted to see to know more about their habits, goals when buying a home, and what their challenges might be during the process of buying a new home. The insights we obtained from these interviews allowed us to prioritize the design of certain features of the website over others.

Persona

"Shopping for a new home there are a few things that we look for in a real estate agency...Optimization, contract help, and commuincation."

Using these recurring themes from these interviews, we created a primary and secondary persona to understand the goals, needs, and pain points of different home buyers who would use a website when shopping for a new home / agency. Jim is in his 30's and just wants an agent that listens to his needs and doesnt try to pressure him into buying something that doesnt make sesne. Jim has been looking for a website that is easy to use and "looks good on his iPhone".

Ideation

Ideation

My team and I used a design studio approach to generate ideas we found this helps us create a more structured approach from the beginning. For the Native Realty website interface, functionality and mobile responsivness were two main focus points for us. We also designed for 3 features that had been identified during user interviews and ranked by the a few of the additional participants.


Initial Prototype:

As Lead UX and Visual Designer, I produced a series of wireframes detailing three primary user flows through the website. These wireframes became our first & second prototype, We tested navigation (hamburger menu on mobile) using Invision, usability, and user perception of key functions such as how to naviagte to the 'workout of the day' from every page on the website.

Wire-Frames

What I Learned

  • Home buyers have a great ammount of options to chose from when looking for a new home, they care about a few things when deciding on the right agency for the job.
  • Users liked seeing the images from listings in the area, but we also found that the users would like a way to reachout to the agency right away.
  • The lead capturing tool on the homepage was both benifical for the client and the home buyers.
  • Users were overwhelmed by the number and size of input fields on the contact form.

What I Changed

  • Initally we had placed a button on the homepage to help direct clients to new listing in their area but this proved to not be very helpful to the agency.
  • We knew that uses wanted to gain more information from their prospective agesnts so we added a tool called JetDesil to help with lead capturing for the client.
  • The importance of the lead capturing tool was both helpful to the client and the user. This was unknown until after the interviews
  • Swapped large form field boxes for smaller, easier to consume fields, mainly to economize space and create some balance between the second CTA and the.

Additional testing

  • We performed 5 usability tests with 3 separate prototypes, gradually improving the fidelity of the screens with each round.
  • This allowed us to test user perceptions of color, images, buttons and branding alongside functionality and useablity.

Solution

  • A clear website that features a lead capturing tool for the client, responsivenss, listing accessibility. Users are enabled to visualize, and comprehend the new features we added in our final iterations, and thus understand their home buying decisions..

Thinking Beyond

  • We received a lot of positive feedback during usability testing as well as interest in the concept of our website. Many users expressed a desire to become a client of Native Realty right away. Considering potential functions, we decided that incorporating a way for our users to communicate with eachother from inside the website would be a wonderful next step, walking scores and other implimentations to help communicate specifc homes with one another.
