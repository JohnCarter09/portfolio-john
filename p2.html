P2 Energy Solutions Design System & Merrick Application

What is P2 Energy Solutions?

All upstream oil and gas organizations strive to be execution-driven, to get oil and gas out of the ground and to market efficiently and profitably. Upstream professionals want to make fast decisions based on reliable data, backed up by robust systems. P2 Energy Solutions empowers people who power the world.

The Goal: To create a Design System that will act as a living, breathing ecosystem of reusable components, guided by clear standards, that can be assembled together to build any number of P2's product offerings, or applications. Another goal was to apply this design system to ship the P2 Merrick application.

   The Process   

Merrick Application Research

  • COMPETITIVE ANALYSIS
  • As a team, we first analyzed several other upstream oil & gas compaines to discover how they went about creating a comprehensive daily, monthly, and transactional application that met the needs of users who were striving to configure their data to meet their localized global requirments.
  • INITIAL RESEARCH
  • In order to understand how to design for our target demographic and the personas we created; Land managers and analysts, we first conducted research on how accountents, Land Managers, and analysts alike, currently use applications in their job when tracking their oil production and productivity. This informed our perception of context as well as the necessity for a well-designed, well performing application.
  • USER INTERVIEWS
  • After screening a few hundred participants from companies that use our product along with other products in the market, we conducted 43 interviews. We saught out to learn more about their habits, goals, and what their challenges might be when facing allocation of their production data. The insights we obtained from these interviews allowed us to prioritize the design of certain features of the new Angular application over others.

"I analyze data that the Field Operators are gathering and make sure every data point is entered correctly. I rectify any data I can if I notice any errors or descripancies. I set up all new tanks, wells, meters and allocation methods."

Using these recurring themes from user interviews, we created a primary persona to understand the goals, needs, and pain points of our main user. Troy works more than 40 hours a week, sometimes 55+ hours, and just wants an application that makes his process more streamlined and easy for his peers to understand when he is presenting. Troy wants a way to be able to view all of his allocations from a daily, monthly, and yearly data set, as this would be easier to 'drill down' into (no pun intended) and view this crucial data.

Ideation

Ideation

My team and I used a design studio approach and tried to used Googles 'Design Sprint' for this project to generate ideas. We found this helps us create a more structured approach from the beginning, where we can all hunker down for a week and really get some good ideas flowing. For the Merrick UI, functionality, readable data tables, were two main focus points for us. We also designed for 3 key features that had been identified during user interviews and ranked by the a few of the additional participants during some of our testing.


Initial Prototype:

As Lead UX and Visual Designer, I produced a series of wireframes detailing three primary user flows through the dashboard of the application. These wireframes became our first & second prototypes before moving into more high fidelity design mockups. We ran some A/B tests on the navigation, profile, and views sections along side functionality of the portlets and graphs, readability, usability, and user perception of these key functions such as how to naviagte to the 'Carte Views' selecetion through a series of differnt flows.

Dashboard Block-frames

Creating wire-frames for Merrick's ProCount applicatiopn was very iteritive between the product teams. Together we made sure this was a very data heavy driven design process from the beginning, (our deliverables had some hard deadlines and we had to get these wires and prototypes out the door and tested on real users very quickly to make sure we were hitting our 1/4 release). Our inital wireframes were lot of block framing (We did this to keep our presnetations concise and direct with stakeholders, insuring that they dont become overwhelmed while we were presenting to them), as well as some of our inital, low fidelity prototypes. We used Mockflow and atomic.io for our wires and porotypes while maintaining the P2' design system principals at processes core. What I Learned

  • In life sometimes you have great days and other times you have bad days, thats part of life. Being comfortable and confident in the face of abiguity is crucial, as a desingers we face ambiguity often. Sometimes when we are gathering requirements, presenting to stakeholders, or testing prototypes things can seem VERY ambiguous, its all about asking the right quesitons during user interviews, failing fast and geting a strong MVP out to client sites for testing. There will always be ups and downs, naturally. Lots of things will be out of your control, so learn to go with the flow. Call out the bad projects for what they are, and enjoy the glory of the good ones. This was something I had to battle with during this project when certain things did not go my way. I have to remind myslef, 'learn from it, get better, and keep pushing forward.'

What I Changed

  • In our inital user testing, we compared the use of graphs verses teh use of data set readability - one question we were asking our selves was, "Are the users using the graph to read through their data or are the users leaning more towards data sets and using pagination to comb throught their data?" We found that users were using the data sets and tables more than the graph so we made the waqy they seatch throguh data in the tables a lot easier.
  • We added more descriptive information about how to access previous data sets that were recently viewed. We changed some of the verbiage from 'last viewed data' to 'Recent Views'
  • We decided that a mega menue would provide less keystrokes for the user trying to navigate to other data sets.

Additional testing

  • We performed 23 usability tests directly, on client sites, with 3 separate prototypes, gradually improving the fidelity of the screens with each round. during our A/B testing - We would mainly use the control and then in a few side by side tests we would add the variation to see how well the user did with it, compared to the control.
  • This allowed us to test user perceptions of color, images, button placement, alongside functionality and useablity.

Solution

  • With the implementation of our P2 design system, it made the progression of our design (for this user story), and work-flow much more concise, from an orginizational stand point. Using the P2 Design system we were able to create strong design principals that, as an orginization, everyone from the top down believed in. A clear application that features allocation methods, responsivenss, and data accessibility. Users are enabled to visualize, and comprehend the new features we added in our final iterations, and thus understand their data sets more clearly allowing our users to make informed decisions when it comes to allocation costs and delivery of the data to their peers, and supervisors for review.

Thinking Beyond

  • We received a lot of feedback during usability testing, some of the feedback was not great. Many users expressed a desire to select a previous entity viewed recenlty. Considering potential functions, we decided that incorporating a way for our users to enhance their own expierence and workflow by adding a mega-menu dropdown feature that would provide a list of different allocations and data sets that were previously viewed so the user did not have to naviage back to another screen to find what they're looking for.
